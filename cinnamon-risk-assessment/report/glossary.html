Privacy Risk
A privacy risk is the possibility that an attacker could extract information about individuals from a released dataset—whether original, anonymized, or synthetic.
Residual privacy risks remain even after applying anonymization or generating synthetic data. These risks must be assessed through attack models (like singling out, linkability, attribute inference).
Singling Out
Singling out occurs when an attacker can deduce that exactly one individual exists in the original dataset who matches a given combination of attribute values.
Data Linkage / Linkability
Linkability is the possibility of linking two or more records—one dataset or across multiple datasets—that belong to the same individual or group of individuals. This can lead to re-identification or information gain about these individuals or the group. Data linkage has historically led to full de-anonymization of datasets and is still possible with synthetic data because of statistical similarities to the real data.
Inference / Attribute Inference
Inference risk arises when an attacker can confidently guess the value of an unknown or secret attribute of a real individual by exploiting an anonymized or synthetic dataset. This can lead to the disclosure of sensitive information like a diagnosis or to identify specifically vulnerable individuals.
Privacy Score (Composite Privacy Risk Score)
A Privacy Score is an aggregation of the three specific privacy risks:
  1. Singling-out Risk
  2. Linkability Risk
  3. Inference (Attribute) Risk
Each of these is computed via the risk score formula for its corresponding attack.
The overall Privacy Score represents a high-level summary of how much the synthetic dataset leaks individual-level information across all key dimensions defined by GDPR and aligned with the Article 29 Working Party. The implementations of the individual risk scores are based on the Anonymeter Framework from Giomi et al.
  • High values mean the synthetic dataset reveals too much about specific individuals.
  • Low values indicate that information leaked is mostly generic population-level information.
Privacy Risk Score
A Privacy Risk Score represents the estimated specific privacy risk for a given attack type (singling out, linkability, inference). It measures how much more successful an attacker is when targeting training records (which influenced the synthetic data) compared to control records (independent of the synthetic data).
Risk Confidence Interval
A Risk Confidence Interval quantifies the statistical uncertainty of the risk estimate due to the finite number of attack trials.
Successful Attack Rate
The Successful Attack Ratio (also referred to as the success rate) is the proportion of attacker guesses that were correct.
Error Rate
In Anonymeter, the Error Rate represents the uncertainty of the success-rate estimate and is expressed as the margin of error from the Wilson score interval.
